*Quotes have the power to change how you fundamentally think.*

These are the words that have shaped how I see the world. Some were plastered across my school walls. Others found me later. All of them stuck.

---

<div style="max-width: 640px; margin: 2rem auto;">

<div style="margin-bottom: 3rem;">
  <blockquote style="font-size: 1.35rem; font-style: italic; border-left: 4px solid #333; padding-left: 1.2rem; margin: 0; line-height: 1.6;">
    "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams lead to thoughts, and thoughts lead to action."
  </blockquote>
  <p style="margin-top: 0.8rem; color: #777; font-size: 0.95rem;">— APJ Abdul Kalam</p>
  <p style="color: #555; line-height: 1.6; margin-top: 0.5rem;">This was on every wall of my school. It's the simplest possible chain of causation: you dream something, the dream becomes a thought, and the thought becomes something you do. Most people skip the dreaming part and wonder why nothing happens.</p>
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom: 3rem;">
  <blockquote style="font-size: 1.35rem; font-style: italic; border-left: 4px solid #333; padding-left: 1.2rem; margin: 0; line-height: 1.6;">
    "कम खाओ, ग़म खाओ, नम जाओ"
  </blockquote>
  <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #555; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.6;">"Eat less, endure hardship, stay humble."</p>
  <p style="margin-top: 0.8rem; color: #777; font-size: 0.95rem;">— Plastered across my school walls</p>
  <p style="color: #555; line-height: 1.6; margin-top: 0.5rem;">Right next to Kalam's words on every wall. Three rules in six words — discipline your body, accept your struggles, and never let your ego outgrow your character.</p>
</div>

</div>
